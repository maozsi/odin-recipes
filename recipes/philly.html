Philly Cheesteak

This Philly Cheesteak is made with sirloin sliced into strips and seasoned with a delicious blend of herbs and spices for a flavorsome sandwich. Feel free to toast the hoagie roll before topping it with the steak, caramelized onions, peppers, and cheese.

Ingredients

Sauce

  • Half teaspoon salt.
  • Half teaspoon black pepper.
  • Half teaspoon paprika.
  • Half teaspoon chili powder.
  • Half teaspoon onion powder.
  • Half teaspoon garlic powder.
  • Half teaspoon dried thyme.
  • Half teaspoon dried marjoram.
  • Half teaspoon dried basil.
  • 1 pound beef sirloin (cut into thin 2 inch strips).
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil.
  • 1 onion, sliced.
  • 1 green bell pepper, julienned.
  • 3 ounches Swiss cheese, thinly sliced.
  • 4 hoagie rolls, split lengthwise.

Instructions

  1. Mix salt, pepper, paprika, chili powder, onion powder, garlic powder, thyme, marjoram, and basil together in a small bowl.
  2. Place steak in a large bowl and sprinkle seasoning mixture over top and stir to coat.
  3. Heat half of the oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Add steak, cook and stir to the desired doneness. Transfer cooked steak to a plate.
  4. Heat the remainder of the oil in the skillet. Add onion and green pepper, cook and stir until tender and caramelized.
  5. Preheat the oven on the broiler setting.
  6. Divide cooked beef between the bottoms of 4 rolls. Layer with onion and green pepper, top with sliced cheese. Place on a cookie sheet.
  7. Broil in the preheated oven until cheese melts.
  8. Cover with tops of rolls and serve.

Enjoy!
